5-Axis CNC Machining Services

Unlocking complex geometries with simultaneous 5-axis precision

What is 5-Axis CNC Machining?

5-axis CNC machining refers to the ability of a CNC machine to move a cutting tool across five different axes simultaneously. Unlike traditional 3-axis machining (X, Y, Z), 5-axis adds two rotary axes (A and B or C), allowing the tool to approach the workpiece from virtually any direction in a single setup.

At Bangnida, we operate 12 advanced 5-axis machining centers from DMG MORI, Mazak, and Haas, with table sizes up to 800mm and spindle speeds to 30,000 RPM.

Types of 5-Axis Machining

Simultaneous 5-Axis (Full 5-Axis)

All five axes move continuously during cutting, enabling complex organic shapes, turbine blades, impellers, and mold cavities with exceptional surface finish.

3+2 Positioning (Indexed 5-Axis)

The two rotary axes position the workpiece at a fixed angle, then the machine cuts in standard 3-axis mode. Ideal for prismatic parts with features on multiple faces — reduces setups from 5-6 to just 1.

Key Benefits of 5-Axis Machining

  • Fewer Setups — Machine complex parts in a single clamping, reducing lead time
  • Better Surface Finish — Shorter, more rigid cutting tools with optimal orientation
  • Complex Geometries — Undercuts, deep cavities, and organic shapes impossible on 3-axis
  • Higher Accuracy — No cumulative error from multiple setups
  • Reduced Fixture Costs — Less complex fixturing needed

FAQ About 5-Axis Machining

While the hourly rate is higher than 3-axis, 5-axis often reduces total part cost by eliminating multiple setups, reducing manual handling, and improving first-pass yield. For complex parts, it is frequently more economical overall.

Choose 5-axis when: your part has features on 4+ faces, requires complex compound angles, needs deep cavities with good surface finish, or has organic/curved surfaces that demand continuous tool orientation.
