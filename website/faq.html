CNC Machining FAQ

Everything you need to know about our precision CNC machining services

Find answers to the most common questions about Bangnida's CNC machining services. If you don't see your question here, contact our team and we'll get back to you within 24 hours.

We accept STEP (.stp, .step), IGES (.igs, .iges), SolidWorks (.sldprt, .sldasm), STL (.stl), DXF (.dxf), PDF (.pdf), and Parasolid (.x_t, .x_b). If you have a different format, please contact our engineering team and we will work with you to import your design.

Our standard machining tolerance is ±0.05 mm (±0.002"). For precision applications, we can hold tolerances down to ±0.005 mm (±0.0002") with CMM inspection on critical features. We work to ISO 2768-m (medium) as a default and can tighten to ISO 2768-f (fine) or custom specifications as required.

No, we have no minimum order quantity. We welcome prototype orders of just 1 part as well as high-volume production runs of millions. From single prototypes to full-scale production, every project receives the same attention to quality and detail.

For express shipping via DHL, FedEx, or UPS, delivery typically takes 3-5 business days worldwide. For sea freight, transit time is approximately 2-4 weeks depending on the destination port. Air freight is also available as a mid-speed option. We ship from our facility in Zhangzhou, Fujian, China.

Yes, we provide full material traceability with mill test reports (MTRs) and certificates of conformance (COC) for all materials used in your parts. Material certifications are included with every shipment at no additional cost, ensuring complete transparency and quality assurance.

We offer a comprehensive range of surface finishes: as-machined (standard 0.8-3.2 Ra), anodizing (Type II and Type III hard coat in various colors), powder coating, electroplating (zinc, nickel, chrome), bead blasting, passivation for stainless steel, polishing, brushing, and painting. Our engineers can recommend the optimal finish for your specific application and environment.

Yes, we offer free samples for evaluation purposes. Simply contact us with your requirements and we will arrange sample machining of your parts. Sample evaluation helps validate our quality, fit, and finish before committing to production orders.

Getting a quote is easy: upload your CAD files through our online quote form, or email your design files to info@bangnida.com. Our engineering team will review your design and respond with a competitive quote within 24 hours, including free DFM (Design for Manufacturing) feedback and material recommendations.

We serve a broad range of industries including: aerospace (turbine blades, structural brackets), automotive (engine components, transmission parts), medical devices (surgical instruments, implantable devices), industrial equipment (hydraulic components, valve bodies), consumer electronics, robotics, energy, oil & gas, defense, and telecommunications.

3-axis machining moves the cutting tool along three linear axes (X, Y, Z) and is ideal for prismatic parts with flat surfaces, drilled holes, and simple geometries. 5-axis machining adds two rotational axes (A and B), allowing the tool to approach the workpiece from virtually any angle. This enables complex geometries, undercuts, organic shapes, and reduces the number of setups required — resulting in better accuracy, superior surface finish, and shorter lead times for complex parts.

Yes, we provide free DFM feedback with every quote. Our experienced engineering team reviews your design and suggests practical optimizations to reduce cost, improve manufacturability, and shorten lead times — such as adjusting overly tight tolerances, modifying feature geometries, adding draft angles, or recommending alternative materials that better suit your application.

We accept T/T (wire transfer) for production orders, L/C (letters of credit) for large-volume or ongoing contracts, and PayPal for sample orders and smaller amounts. Payment terms are flexible and can be tailored to your company's procurement policies. For established partners, net terms may be available upon request.

Quality is ensured through our ISO 9001:2015 certified quality management system. Our process includes: 100% first-article inspection (FAI) for new projects, in-process inspection at every manufacturing stage, final inspection with full dimensional reports, CMM (Coordinate Measuring Machine) inspection for critical features, surface roughness measurement, and material certifications traceable to source. We maintain 25+ full-time QC staff across 6 CMM machines.

Yes, we can machine parts from customer-supplied material. This is a common option for clients with specialized materials, proprietary alloys, or existing stock they want to use. Please provide material specifications and certifications with your shipment so we can verify suitability for the machining process.

Typical lead times are 3-7 business days for prototypes and 7-15 business days for production quantities. Expedited service is available for urgent projects. Actual lead time depends on part complexity, quantity, material availability, and current production capacity. We will confirm the schedule with your quote.

Yes, we routinely sign NDAs to protect your intellectual property. Your designs and proprietary information are kept strictly confidential. We can sign your company's NDA or provide our standard NDA — whichever you prefer. All digital files are transmitted via secure channels.

Absolutely. We machine individual components and can supply fully assembled parts with hardware insertion, welding, and functional testing. Our team manages complex bills of materials (BOMs) and delivers kitted assemblies ready for your production line, reducing your supply chain complexity.
